Hats for babies in Ukraine

There is so much we can learn from each other

Often considered an adornment, hats for newborn babies may be life- saving. Their heads are relatively larger than bodies compared to older children and adults and a large source of heat loss. Mild increased heat loss may be associated with poor feeding and growth. Larger losses may be associated with decreased body temperature and increased chance of death.

Hats are often knitted, especially but not exclusively by grandmothers, sometimes along with booties and mittens which are also useful. For the less experienced, instructions are available.

We’ve supplied 10,000 knitted hats and a large quantity of baby clothes to maternity units in Ukraine, thanks to the generosity of UK Rotarians and friends.

We are no longer able to collect or transport such items to Ukraine.
